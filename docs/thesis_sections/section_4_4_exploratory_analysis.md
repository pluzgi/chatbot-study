## 4.4 Exploratory Analysis

### Dashboard Configuration Preferences

Participants in high-control conditions (C and D, n = 102) configured their data donation via the Granular Consent Dashboard. Chi-square tests revealed no significant differences between Conditions C and D for any dashboard dimension (all p > .38, all V < .14). The most common configuration was full data scope (73.5%), academic-only purpose (67.6%), Swiss server storage (47.1%), and retention until purpose fulfilled (59.8%). Notably, 68.6% preferred Swiss or EU server storage, suggesting data sovereignty concerns.

### Open-Ended Feedback Analysis

The 120 open-ended responses to Q14 ("What mattered most for your data donation decision?") were analyzed using deterministic multilingual keyword-based content analysis. Ten themes were derived inductively from reading all responses. For each theme, keyword lists were compiled in German, French, and English — the three primary participant languages. Coding was performed through case-insensitive substring matching, with multi-label assignment (responses could match multiple themes). This approach ensures full reproducibility without reliance on manual or LLM-based coding. The codebook achieved 93.3% coverage (112/120 responses matched at least one theme; M = 1.50 themes per response).

### Condition Contrasts

Pairwise comparisons revealed descriptive patterns in how experimental manipulations shaped participant reasoning (differences ≥ 5 pp reported):

**Adding the dashboard (A → C)** produced the largest single shift: *control/choice* increased by +26.7 pp (10.3% → 37.0%), indicating participants noticed and valued the control mechanism.

**Adding the DNL to the dashboard (C → D)** reversed this emphasis: *control/choice* dropped −27.0 pp, while *support research* (+15.2 pp) and *data purpose* (+12.2 pp) increased — suggesting the DNL shifted attention from the act of choosing to the substance of what was being disclosed.

**Adding the DNL alone (A → B)** increased mentions of *data purpose* (+10.8 pp) and *trust in institution* (+9.7 pp).

**Adding the dashboard to DNL (B → D)** increased *support research* (+21.2 pp) and *data storage/security* (+11.6 pp), while reducing *trust in institution* (−16.9 pp).

Due to the ceiling effect in donation rates (91.7%), only 5 respondents had declined, precluding meaningful comparison by donation decision.

*Note. These condition contrasts are descriptive and exploratory; they should not be interpreted as confirmatory evidence.*
