## 4.2 Manipulation Checks

The manipulation checks assess whether the experimental conditions produced the intended psychological states. Two manipulation checks were conducted: perceived transparency (MC-T) to validate the transparency manipulation (H1), and perceived control (MC-C) to validate the control manipulation (H2). Following the measurement plan, Shapiro-Wilk tests were used to assess normality; when normality assumptions were violated, Mann-Whitney U tests were employed with rank-biserial r as the matched effect size.

**Perceived Transparency (MC-T).** The transparency manipulation was assessed by comparing participants' perceived transparency scores between low-transparency conditions (T0: Conditions A and C) and high-transparency conditions (T1: Conditions B and D). Shapiro-Wilk tests indicated non-normal distributions in both groups (T0: W = 0.87, p < .001; T1: W = 0.87, p < .001), so a Mann-Whitney U test was conducted.

**Table 7**
*Manipulation Check for Perceived Transparency (MC-T)*

| Group | Conditions | n | M | SD |
|-------|------------|---|---|----|
| T0 (Low Transparency) | A, C | 95 | 4.67 | 1.24 |
| T1 (High Transparency) | B, D | 110 | 4.83 | 1.09 |

The Mann-Whitney U test did not reveal a significant difference between transparency conditions, U = 5,518.0, p = .480. Although participants in high-transparency conditions (T1) reported slightly higher perceived transparency (M = 4.83, SD = 1.09) than those in low-transparency conditions (T0: M = 4.67, SD = 1.24), this difference was negligible (rank-biserial r = 0.06, 95% CI [-0.08, 0.19]). The Data Nutrition Label did not significantly increase perceived transparency.

**Perceived Control (MC-C).** The control manipulation was assessed by comparing participants' perceived control scores between low-control conditions (C0: Conditions A and B) and high-control conditions (C1: Conditions C and D). Shapiro-Wilk tests indicated non-normal distributions (C0: W = 0.93, p < .001; C1: W = 0.89, p < .001), so a Mann-Whitney U test was conducted.

**Table 8**
*Manipulation Check for Perceived Control (MC-C)*

| Group | Conditions | n | M | SD |
|-------|------------|---|---|----|
| C0 (Low Control) | A, B | 103 | 4.35 | 1.34 |
| C1 (High Control) | C, D | 102 | 4.84 | 0.95 |

The Mann-Whitney U test revealed a significant difference between control conditions, U = 6,325.0, p = .010. Participants in high-control conditions (C1) reported significantly higher perceived control (M = 4.84, SD = 0.95) than those in low-control conditions (C0: M = 4.35, SD = 1.34). The effect size was small (rank-biserial r = 0.20, 95% CI [0.07, 0.33]), indicating that the Granular Consent Dashboard effectively increased perceived control.

**Summary.** The manipulation check for control (MC-C) passed, confirming that the Granular Consent Dashboard successfully increased perceived control. However, the manipulation check for transparency (MC-T) did not reach statistical significance, suggesting that the Data Nutrition Label did not produce a detectable increase in perceived transparency. This partial manipulation failure has implications for interpreting the transparency hypothesis (H1) results in subsequent analyses.
